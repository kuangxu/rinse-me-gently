Developing a New Wearable Device

Fitbit, Inc. is a wearable technology company headquartered in San Francisco, California. The company was founded in 2007 by James Park and Eric Friedman, with the goal of inspiring individuals to live healthier and more active lives. Fitbit specializes in fitness trackers that measure data such as the number of steps walked, quality of sleep, and heart rate. They have sold over 90 million devices, and the company is considered one of the leaders in the wearables market. 

This case focuses on decisions Fitbit faces in developing and releasing a new product that does much more than activity tracking. However, they have reasons to believe that some of their biggest competitors are also developing similar products. By making careful decisions about the product with the help of some modeling in Excel, Fitbit believes they can still dominate the market for this new type of device.

Producing more devices lowers the cost per device, but also increases the total time it takes for the devices to arrive from the manufacturer. Delivery time, on the other hand, is determined by the last device to be produced. For example, a production order of 80,000 would mean that ALL devices will be delivered after 11 weeks.  

Fitbit wants to release their device as soon as possible, since they expect their competitor to enter the market any day now. Therefore, they have decided to order the devices today, and they are debating what shipping date to announce to their customers (how many weeks from today). If the products arrive from the manufacturer by the announced shipping date, they can be shipped to the customers who have pre-ordered the device without any complications. However, for every additional week that the devices are delayed beyond the announced shipping date, Fitbit has decided to give each customer who has pre-ordered the device a $25 rebate. So, if they announce a shipping date of 8 weeks from today, but the devices take 9 weeks to be produced, Fitbit will give each customer a $25 rebate. If they take 10 weeks to be produced, Fitbit will give each customer a $50 rebate.
